Abstract

Thirteen open-source text-to-speech models were evaluated against AWS Nova Sonic v2 as a reference for production-grade real-time voice AI. Testing was conducted locally on a Mac where possible, with the same input text passed to each model. Quality was assessed across naturalness, latency, local viability, voice cloning, emotion control, and licensing.

The most striking finding: Kokoro-82M (82M params, <few hundred hours of training data) was rated "very natural — surprising" — outperforming fishspeech which trained on 2M+ hours and 0.5B parameters. Architecture and data curation consistently dominated raw scale. VibeVoice-Realtime-0.5B running locally at ~300ms was rated "almost the same feel as Nova Sonic 2." The cloud gap is closing.
